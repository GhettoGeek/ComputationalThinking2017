9 lectures on Computational Thinking by Joel Gethin Lewis.

  • Lecture 1: Introduction. The History of Graphic Design and the History of Graphic Computers. What Computational Thinking is and why it's important to everyone (and for Graphic Designers). How to count (in binary).
  • Lecture 2: Form. Matching socks and finding your size. Introduction to p5.js. Variables and Types.
  • Lecture 3: Colour and Typography. Popping to the shops and finding your way home. Lists and arrays. Introduction to images in p5.js.
  • Lecture 4: Grids and Logos. Sort that post and be more hip. Loops and comments. Introduction to p5.js libraries.
  • Lecture 5: Randomisation. Updating your status and getting jobs done. Functions. How to load an animated gif.
  • Lecture 6: Repetition. Fixing necklaces. Classes. How to make a new animated gif class. How to use search engines.
  • Lecture 7: Transformation. Locating boxes. How to output an animated gif with a GUI library. How to ask on forums.
  • Lecture 8: Motion. Filling shelves. How to mess with images or convolutions. How to steal and share. How to use GitHub.
  • Lecture 9: 3D. Navigating aisles. How to get live camera input working. How to make projects. How to think about things. The resources that I use.
